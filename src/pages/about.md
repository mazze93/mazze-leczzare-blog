Content strategist, security researcher, and the founder of
[Secure Pride](https://securepride.org), a privacy-first cybersecurity nonprofit built
to serve LGBTQ+ organizations and the communities they protect.

Most of my work lives at the intersection of language and infrastructure. On any given
week I might be writing positioning strategy for a developer platform, designing a
certificate lifecycle for a wireless network that can't afford to fail, or figuring out
how a nonprofit with no IT budget can still have enterprise-grade security. The problems
look different. The underlying question is always the same: *who is this for, and what
do they actually need?*

---

## Background

I studied cognitive neuroscience and quantitative psychology at UC Davis, where I worked
in the Luck Lab on EEG/ERP research, eye tracking, and neural network modeling. That
work didn't pull me away from writing — it sharpened how I think about attention,
signal, and cognitive load, which turn out to matter as much in security design as they
do in content strategy.

Before Secure Pride, I led content at mudstack, produced the *Clear as Mud* podcast
(fifty-plus episodes, with guests from Epic, Riot, Sony, and Meta VR), and supported
growth work at Tennis 919. The common thread: systems thinking applied to communication,
and a refusal to treat "good enough" as a real category.

## What I Build

**Secure Pride** is the project I'm most proud of. It exists because LGBTQ+ organizations
handle deeply sensitive data — identity disclosures, health information, community
trust — and often operate without the resources to protect it. I'm building the
infrastructure, the education layer, and the cultural competency frameworks to close
that gap.

**Context Synapse** is a local-first context orchestration framework in Swift. It's a
Bayesian system for managing what an AI model knows about you across time, without ever
sending that data anywhere. Research project and values statement, simultaneously.

**Merchants of War** is the speculative fiction I write when I need to think in a
different register — a world-building project about power, labor, and the aesthetics
of constructed things.
